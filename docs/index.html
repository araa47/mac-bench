Why this exists

I bought a Mac mini (M4 Pro, 64 GB) to run Home Assistant and handle everything locally — no cloud, no subscriptions, no sending camera feeds to someone else's servers. The first thing I wanted was a vision model that could look at my doorbell camera and tell me "there's a person with a package at the door" in a sentence or two.

I figured I'd benchmark a few tiny local models before buying any extra hardware, just to see if any of them were even usable. Turns out, some of them are surprisingly good. A 3-billion-parameter model describes doorbell snapshots in under 3 seconds, using less than 3 GB of RAM. That was enough to make me want to benchmark everything I could find.

Face recognition was the natural next step — if the camera can describe who it sees, can it also tell me who that person is? InsightFace nailed 100% accuracy on the LFW verification pairs, running locally in under 200ms per comparison. All on the same Mac mini, no GPU required.

Source code on GitHub. Built with local models, for local models.
